### Built for PCB Manufacturing Processes

PCB labels may encounter high heat, flux, solder exposure, aggressive cleaning chemistry and repeated wash cycles—all before the finished assembly enters service. A suitable construction must balance face material, adhesive, print topcoat and ribbon performance for the actual process.

Polyonics polyimide materials cover different requirements rather than relying on one universal construction. ETIA helps compare the process route, ESD controls, label profile, surface energy, print method and cleaning conditions before recommending a material.

### Choose by Process Requirement

- **General low-profile PCB identification:** XF-533
- **Ultra-high-heat amber identification:** XF-542
- **Ultra-thin ESD control:** XF-101SE, XF-101ME or XF-101M
- **ESD identification with a more substantial construction:** XF-102SE
- **Harsh-wash printing without a preheat/reflow step:** XF-102G
- **Color-coded high-temperature identification:** XF-505 light green

### Application Areas

Printed circuit board traceability; SMT and through-hole processing; wave solder and reflow workflows; electronic component pre-process identification; work-in-process barcodes; serial-number and lot tracking; ESD-controlled electronics manufacturing.

### Selection Matters More Than Size

Label dimensions can be converted to suit the available marking area. Material selection should come first. Confirm peak temperature and dwell time, direct solder exposure, cleaning chemistry, number of wash cycles, ESD requirements, surface type, barcode density and ribbon compatibility.

### Why ETIA

ETIA supports material selection, samples, format conversion and supply planning. Product availability, printer compatibility and regulatory status can vary by region and configuration; confirm the final construction for the intended process before production.
